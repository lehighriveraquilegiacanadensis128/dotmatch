# Code of Conduct

DotMatch is intended to be useful to computational biologists, bioinformaticians, software engineers, and method developers. Keep discussion technical, specific, and respectful.

## Expected Behavior

- Assume good intent, but be precise about claims, limitations, and evidence.
- Critique code, benchmarks, documentation, and scientific framing directly without personal attacks.
- Respect differences in expertise across biology, statistics, software engineering, and operations.
- Keep discussions on topic and make issues actionable.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or sustained personal attacks.
- Dismissive comments about a person's background, identity, institution, or level of experience.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Deliberately misleading benchmark, citation, or authorship claims.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues; block accounts; or reject contributions that violate this code. For sensitive concerns, use a private maintainer contact path rather than a public issue.
